Vegetable Biryani

An exquisite culinary journey that transforms humble vegetables into a masterpiece of flavor and aroma. Each layer tells a story of spices, texture, and traditional cooking techniques.

Spice Level

Medium

Cooking Time

50-65 minutes

Serving Size

4-6 persons

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • Mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans)
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 green cardamoms
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 3-4 cloves
  • 2 tbsp ghee or oil
  • 1/4 cup milk (saffron)
  • 2 tbsp mint leaves
  • 2 tbsp coriander leaves
  • Salt to taste

Cooking Process

Step 1: Rice Preparation

Soak basmati rice for 30 minutes. Boil water with whole spices, add rice, and cook until 80% done. Drain and set aside.

Step 2: Vegetable Prep

Chop mixed vegetables. Parboil them to retain crispness and vibrant colors. Set aside.

Step 3: Masala Magic

Heat ghee in a pan, fry onions until golden. Add ginger-garlic paste, tomatoes, and spices. Cook until oil separates.

Step 4: Vegetable Integration

Add parboiled vegetables to the masala. Mix well and cook for 5-7 minutes.

Step 5: Layering Artistry

Layer cooked vegetables, add rice, sprinkle mint, coriander, and saffron milk. Repeat layers.

Step 6: Final Flourish

Cover tightly, cook on low heat for 15-20 minutes. Let it rest for 10 minutes before serving.

Don't forget add chilli if you are a spice lover
