Hyderabadi Mutton Biryani

A Royal Culinary Journey of Flavors and Traditions

Ingredients

  • 500g mutton (bone-in)
  • ¬Ω cup yogurt
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • ¬Ω tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp mustard oil (optional)
  • 2 cups basmati rice (soaked for 30 minutes)
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2-3 green cardamoms
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 3-4 cloves
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp ghee or oil
  • 1 large onion (thinly sliced)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 green chili (slit)
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • ¬Ω tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • ¬Ω tsp garam masala
  • ¬º cup milk (with a pinch of saffron soaked)
  • 2 tbsp chopped coriander leaves
  • 2 tbsp chopped mint leaves
  • 1 tbsp kewra or rose water (optional)

Cooking Process

  1. Step 1: Marinate the Mutton (Minimum 2 Hours or Overnight)

    In a bowl, mix mutton, yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, salt, lemon juice, and mustard oil. Cover and let it marinate in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours (overnight is best for tender meat).

  2. Step 2: Cook the Rice

    Rinse 2 cups basmati rice and soak it for 30 minutes. Boil 4 cups water in a large pot. Add bay leaves, cardamoms, cinnamon, cloves, and salt. Add soaked rice and cook until 70-80% done (it should still have a bite). Drain and set aside.

  3. Step 3: Prepare the Biryani Masala

    Heat 2 tbsp ghee/oil in a large pot. Add sliced onions and fry until golden brown (caramelized). Set aside some for garnishing. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1 minute until fragrant. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft. Add red chili powder, turmeric, coriander, cumin, and salt. Cook for a few minutes. Add the marinated mutton and cook on medium heat until the oil separates. Add ¬Ω cup water, cover, and simmer for 40-50 minutes (or pressure cook for 4-5 whistles) until the mutton is tender.

  4. Step 4: Layering the Biryani

    In the same pot with cooked mutton, add a layer of half the rice. Sprinkle fried onions, coriander leaves, mint leaves, and saffron milk. Add the remaining rice on top and drizzle ghee, kewra/rose water for aroma.

  5. Step 5: Dum Cooking (Final Cooking)

    Cover the pot with a tight lid or seal with dough. Cook on low heat (dum) for 20-25 minutes to let the flavors infuse. Turn off the heat and let it rest for 10 minutes before opening.

  6. Step 6: Serve & Enjoy!

    Gently mix the layers while serving. Garnish with reserved fried onions, mint, and coriander. Serve hot with raita or salan.

    7. Enjoy your delicious Hyderabadi Mutton Biryani!
